  1. Electronic commerce, in short, E-commerce, has repidly evolved since its creation. At first it was in the form of Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) in the 1960s, this allowed businesses to share their documents electronically. The use of early e-commerce ended up developing a future in online transactions. According to Investopedia, EDI produced a simple exchange of business documents such as invoices or purchase orders. This phenomenon helped streamlining operations and reduced paperwork for many. After many advancements in technology, online banking and electronic funds transfers were made accessible, extentuating the success of e-commerce. Furthermore, the secure socket layer (SSL) encryption was introduced in the 1990s. This provided more security of online transactions, helping consumers to trust e-commerce platforms with their personal information. As well as this, the rise of personal mobile devices in the 21st century helped with mobile commerce (m-commerce). This allows consumers to shop conveniently from their smartphones. With this evolution, e-commerce has become a very sophisticated system, encompassing a various range of online transactions.

  2. " The progression of the Internet in the 1990's also became a significant milestone in e-commerce. This revolution helped the transition from traditional commerce to online retail. To start off, companies like Amazon and eBay emerged successfully. These sites introduced concepts such as online marketplaces including secure payment systems. This changed the retail landscape (BigCommerce). According to BigCommerce, the internet provided a major global platform for businesses to reach customers beyond geographical boundaries. With this evolution entrepreneurship became more popular, individuals began to set up online stores with minimal investment, as well as minimal overhead costs (BigCommerce). In addition to this, there were many advancements in website design, as well as user experience enhancement making online shopping more enjoyable for consumers. The introduction of social media platforms also added to the success of e-commerce. Social media enables businesses to utilize social networks for marketing, as well as customer engagement (BigCommerce). As a result, e-commerce has become an major part of the modern economy, with online sales making up a significant portion of retail transactions around the world."
